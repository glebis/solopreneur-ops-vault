### What's Getting Traction on LinkedIn This Week

1. **Agentic workflows** — still the dominant theme. Andrew Ng's content in May 2026 focuses heavily on this; his framing sets the industry vocabulary.
2. **No-code AI agents for business professionals** — Justin Shaifer and similar voices are growing. The "non-technical" framing is increasingly mainstream.
3. **Alex Wang (GenAI.Works)** — building serious traction with "90% buzzword-free" AI education content. Accessible tone, high signal. Worth studying his format.
4. **AI literacy as a policy issue** — Boston Public Schools announced AI literacy as a **high school graduation requirement** starting September 2026 (backed by $1M grant). This is major: it mainstream-ifies the conversation and validates non-technical AI education.
5. **Carousels + polls** are the highest-engagement format on LinkedIn for AI educators in 2026 (per Metricool study).
6. **Ethical AI** emerging as a content pillar — copyright, data privacy, responsible use. Not just "how to use" but "how to use responsibly."
