## New Competitors / Courses

### Matt Pocock — AI Hero v2 Goes Tool-Agnostic (Major Shift)

This is the biggest competitor move this week. Matt Pocock announced **AI Coding for Real Engineers** — effectively AI Hero cohort v2 — with several significant changes:

| Detail | v1 (April cohort) | v2 (June 1 cohort) |
|--------|------------------|--------------------|
| **Price** | $795 | **$696** (30% off, sale ends May 26) |
| **Tool focus** | Claude Code–specific | **Any coding agent** (Cursor, Copilot, Windsurf, etc.) |
| **Enrollment** | ~2,500 students last cohort | New cohort opens |
| **Dates** | April | **June 1–12** (2 weeks) |
| **Office hours** | 2 sessions | Jun 1, Jun 8, Jun 12 (two TZ-friendly slots each) |

**The critical signal:** Matt explicitly pivoted to agent-agnostic framing after 2,500 students in his Claude Code cohort. He's chasing a wider market — but this opens a gap. He is no longer "the Claude Code educator for engineers." That positioning is now more available.

**Implication for Claude Code Lab:** The 2-week, developer-only, self-directed format is now explicitly positioned *away from* our 6-week, cohort-supported, non-dev-inclusive model. The head-to-head comparison becomes easier to write: we are the longer, deeper, non-developer-friendly alternative with 50+ skills included.

**Watch:** The May 26 sale deadline is a pressure tactic worth studying. $696 → presumably back to $795 after. Monitor whether the price reverts or this becomes the new standard.

### AI Product Academy — May Promotion Running

Dr. Marily Nika's AI PM Bootcamp is running a **$1,000 off / buy 1 get 1** promotion in May 2026. The refreshed May curriculum includes OpenClaw, Claude Code, and n8n automation. Price post-promo: ~$2,999 per person. Still PM-only, still hardware-inclusive, still 5× our price.

**Implication:** The promotion signals softer demand than expected for a $3K course. They're moving seats with discounts. No change to our competitive position.

### Class Central — "Best AI Agent Courses for Non-Coders" Roundup

Class Central published a roundup: "6 Best AI Agent Courses for Beginners and Non-Coders in 2026." This creates an aggregated discovery surface for our exact target audience. None of the six are 6-week cohort-depth offerings — most are self-paced or 1-2 weeks. We are not listed (yet).

**Opportunity:** Getting listed on Class Central roundups is a distribution win. Their search traffic from non-technical learners is significant.

### Penn GSE — Institutional AI Entry

University of Pennsylvania's Graduate School of Education launched "Implementing AI in the Classroom" — a Summer 2026 cohort at **$2,050 for 4 months online**. Not a direct competitor (targets K-12 teachers), but signals that academic institutions are now pricing AI education in the $2K range and moving fast.

## Tool Updates

### Claude Code — "Code with Claude" May 21 Event Fallout

The MIT Technology Review called Code with Claude "a look at coding's future — whether you like it or not." Key releases now shipping (or newly visible to the public):

| Feature | Description | Teaching Angle |
|---------|-------------|---------------|
| **Routines** | Automated coding workflows on schedules, API calls, or webhooks — run on Claude's cloud infrastructure | New session topic: "your agent that works while you sleep" |
| **GitHub Webhook Routines** | Claude Code sessions auto-launch when PRs match conditions; monitors comments, CI failures, lifecycle | Advanced automation demo for developer students |
| **Desktop App Redesign** | Session sidebar, drag-and-drop workspace, integrated terminal + file editor, faster diffs, expanded previews, SSH on Mac | Better live demo experience in cohorts |
| **Managed Agents: Outcomes + Webhooks** | Agents return structured outcomes; trigger downstream workflows via webhooks | Multi-agent orchestration demo |
| **Expanded API limits** | Claude Code and Opus limits raised again | Less friction during intensive cohort sessions |

**Routines is the biggest new teaching opportunity.** It's the bridge from "I use Claude Code manually" to "I have a system that runs automatically." This is the solopreneur automation story made concrete — and nobody is teaching it yet.

### Anthropic Routines — InfoQ Coverage

Anthropic's Routines feature landed formal coverage on InfoQ (May 2026), signaling it's production-ready, not preview. The framing: developers configure workflows that trigger on schedules or events; Claude handles execution on cloud infra. No local machine required to keep agents running.

**For Claude Code Lab:** This is a Skill waiting to be built. A `setup-routine` skill that scaffolds a student's first automated workflow would be a standout cohort deliverable.

### MCP Ecosystem — Stability Improving

Ecosystem size holds at 10,000+ public servers, but the focus is shifting from quantity to quality. Notion, Stripe, Cloudflare, Slack, and Vercel now have **official** MCP servers (vendor-maintained, not community experiments). Google Colab shipped its own MCP server in April.

**Practical ceiling:** Multiple sources now cite 5–7 simultaneous MCP servers as the working maximum before tool-selection accuracy degrades. This validates our curated 50-skill approach over breadth.

## Content Trends

### What's Getting Traction This Week

1. **Routines / automated agents** — the concept of "agents that run while you're offline" is generating high engagement across LinkedIn and X. Anyone posting practical examples (not theory) is getting significant reach.

2. **Matt Pocock's tool-agnostic pivot** — developers are discussing whether Claude Code vs. Cursor vs. Copilot matters anymore. The meta-question ("which coding agent should I use?") is traffic bait right now.

3. **"Agentic solopreneur" framing** — content about running a one-person business *with* AI agents (not just using AI tools) is resonating. Vocal Media, Futurism, and LinkedIn creator accounts are picking up this framing. Our positioning here is ahead of the curve.

4. **No-code vs. Claude Code debate** — Harold Dijkstra's no-code positioning and Matt Pocock's engineering-first positioning represent the two poles. There's a content vacuum in the middle: "I'm not a developer but I want to go deeper than no-code." That's our audience, and almost nobody is speaking directly to them.

5. **Carousels + polls** remain the highest-engagement LinkedIn format for AI educators. Carousels showing "before vs. after" agent automation journeys are particularly strong.

### Emerging Topic: GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)

Class Central and other course roundup sites are publishing "Best GEO Courses in 2026" content. GEO — optimizing content for AI-native search (Claude, Perplexity, ChatGPT search) — is a genuinely new topic with almost no structured courses. Adjacent to AI education but not our core. Worth watching as a potential content angle for the newsletter.

## Industry News

- **US Department of Education: $169M FIPSE grant** for responsible AI use in postsecondary education. Funds AI integration across accreditation, teaching, and short-term programs. Creates budget at universities for external AI training partnerships — potential enterprise channel.

- **Google AI Opportunity Fund:** $37M total, now funding 4.7M learners across 19 countries in Asia-Pacific. Big-tech top-of-funnel work benefits independent educators downstream (more aware buyers).

- **Boston BPS AI literacy mandate (confirmed Sept 2026):** First major US city requiring AI fluency for high school graduation, backed by $1M grant. As of this week, NYC parents are pushing back on AI deployments, creating a policy tension story. Both sides are creating media coverage that normalizes "AI education" as a topic.

- **Edtech VC funding remains modest:** 21 deals, $183.6M raised (May 2025–Apr 2026). No rounds over $50M. This is *good* for independent educators — the space isn't being flooded with VC-backed competitors.

- **MIT Technology Review on Code with Claude:** Major mainstream media validation that agentic coding is a real shift, not hype. Their framing ("whether you like it or not") signals we're past early-adopter phase and entering the mainstream professional conversation.
