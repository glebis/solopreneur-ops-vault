## Content Trends

### LinkedIn Algorithm (2026 Update — LinkedIn 360 Brew AI)

LinkedIn replaced thousands of task-specific models with a unified AI system (360 Brew AI) in early 2026. Key changes with direct implications:

**What changed:**
- **Dwell time over likes.** A post read for 30 seconds outranks a post with 50 quick likes. The algorithm measures time-on-content, not reaction count.
- **Organic reach down 50%** for creators still using 2024-era tactics (hashtag density, engagement pods).
- **External links: 60% reach penalty.** Posts with links in the body see significantly reduced distribution. Links-in-comments rule is now even more important.
- **Document posts (PDF carousels) lead all formats** at 6.60% average engagement — driven by forced swipe interactions generating dwell time.
- **Video growth: +36% YoY.** Native video now at 5.60% engagement. Captions are confirmed 2.3x multiplier.
- **Authentic expertise signals** replace engagement volume as the key ranking input.

## New Concepts

### 1. Dwell Time Architecture

**The idea:** LinkedIn's 360 Brew AI weights dwell time as the primary signal. This means posts should be designed not just to stop the scroll (hook) but to hold attention through structure. "Dwell time architecture" is a framework for writing posts with deliberate pacing: hook → tension → payoff cadence, with white space and line breaks optimised for reading time, not reading speed.

**Why it matters:** The existing [[frameworks/LinkedIn Hook Patterns]] addresses the first 2 lines (click bait) but doesn't address the 30-second reading window. A Dwell Time Architecture framework would complete the post design picture.

**Outline:**
- Structure: Hook (2 lines) → Tension (3–4 lines) → Evidence (2–3 lines) → Payoff (1–2 lines) → CTA (optional)
- White space rule: no paragraph longer than 3 lines on mobile
- Dwell boosters: numbered lists, one-sentence paragraphs, partial reveals
- Measurement: track comments-per-impression (proxy for read rate) vs likes-per-impression

**Candidate file:** `frameworks/Dwell Time Architecture.md`

### 2. Proof of Human Insight (PoHI)

**The idea:** As AI content floods every feed, the scarcest signal is demonstrable human experience that AI cannot generate: specific cohort data, exact alumni outcomes, real failure stories with named consequences. "Proof of Human Insight" is a content credentialing framework — every post should contain at least one PoHI marker that makes it recognisably the product of lived experience.

**Why it matters:** The Ramp March 2026 data (Claude wins 70% of head-to-head matchups among first-time buyers) suggests Claude is the professional default. An AI educator whose content could itself be generated by Claude is invisible. PoHI is the differentiator.

**PoHI Markers:**
- Specific numbers from real events ("Week 3 is when 60% of cohort students hit their first agent that works")
- Named alumni outcomes with their permission
- Personal failure with named cost ("I lost the Amsterdam lead by waiting 3 days to follow up")
- Contrarian data from your own cohort that contradicts the general advice

**Candidate file:** `frameworks/Proof of Human Insight.md`
