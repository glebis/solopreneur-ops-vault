## Content Trends

### 1. Depth Score Confirmed as Primary LinkedIn Signal

The July 2026 algorithm data confirms and extends the dwell-time signal reported in prior reviews. LinkedIn's 360Brew system now explicitly surfaces this as a "Depth Score" — the measured ratio of time-on-content to impressions.

Key confirmed data points:
- A post someone reads for 30 seconds outperforms one with 50 quick likes
- PDF carousels force swipe interactions that accumulate dwell time natively; hitting **6.60% engagement** — still the highest of any format
- Videos under **90 seconds** perform best for completion rate; longer videos lose the algorithm's dwell-time credit before the payoff lands
- Text posts with no visual: ~2% engagement ceiling

**For Claude Code Lab content:** Short-form talking-head video (demo clip, not lecture) under 90 seconds is now the underused format. The LinkedIn Hook Patterns framework covers carousel and text; it does not specify video length. Framework updated below.

### 2. Topic Authority — Narrow Topic Consistency Now Explicitly Rewarded

360Brew introduces a "Topic Authority" model: the algorithm learns what you post about and routes new posts to that established interest graph. Posting consistently about a narrow topic amplifies reach over time; posting inconsistently across multiple topics resets distribution credit with each post.

**Implication:** The "80/20 educational vs promotional" rule is correct but incomplete. The deeper constraint is topic-depth-over-time: an AI educator who posts consistently about Claude Code non-developer use cases builds topic authority the algorithm will amplify to new audiences. An AI educator who posts about productivity, business strategy, AI tools, parenting, and climate science distributes to no consistent interest graph.

**Vault gap:** This is the fifth time "Topic DNA Cultivation" has been raised in a review. It has not been created as a framework. **Created this session** — see `frameworks/Topic DNA Cultivation.md`.

### 3. Solopreneur AI Adoption Surge — Education Gap Widening

New July 2026 data:
- US solopreneur AI adoption has reached **~89%** of the solo business segment (up from ~23% in mid-2025)
- 74% of solopreneurs using AI report **task-level speed gains** but not structural time reduction
- McKinsey data: **73% of solopreneurs who try AI automation abandon it within 90 days** — not because the tools don't work, but because they never build repeatable workflows around them
- The Fortune "solo founders doing the work of entire teams" coverage is generating LinkedIn discourse; most replies are tool recommendations, not system design guidance

**Counter-narrative opportunity (high priority):** The 73% abandonment figure is the strongest objection-handling data point Claude Code Lab has ever had. The cohort isn't teaching tools — it is teaching the workflow design that prevents the 73% outcome. A single LinkedIn post framing this ("73% of solopreneurs drop AI within 90 days — here's the design pattern that keeps you in the 27%") has all the structural elements of a viral post: specific stat, contrarian framing, promised payoff.

### 4. "Build in Public" + Process Content Rising

The July content trend data confirms "build in public" as a high-engagement LinkedIn content format for solopreneur educators. Publishing real in-progress work (screenshots, vault structure, Claude Code sessions, real alumni projects) outperforms polished explainer content on authenticity signals. LinkedIn's March 2026 Authenticity Update penalises generic AI-generated text; live-session clips and real-work screenshots are structurally immune to this filter.

**For Claude Code Lab:** The current content calendar is framework-heavy (carousels, structured posts). Build-in-public content requires almost no preparation — screenshot a real session, caption it, publish. This is the lowest-effort high-signal content type that is not currently in the calendar.

### 5. Personal AI Twin Framing — Entering the Mainstream Vocabulary

"Personal AI twin" — a deeply personalized agent that internalises your thinking patterns, values, and expertise — is now appearing in LinkedIn Learning and major educator vocabulary. The skills library session in Claude Code Lab is already doing this technically. The vocabulary has not been adopted.

**Quick win:** Update the marketing framing for the skills library session. "By Session 12 you have 50+ skills — a digital twin that knows your workflow and handles your repeatable tasks without supervision." Same curriculum, more compelling outcome narrative.

## New Concepts

### 1. AI Workflow Abandonment Pattern

**The idea:** McKinsey data confirms that 73% of solopreneurs who try AI automation abandon it within 90 days. The failure mode is not tool selection — it is workflow architecture. Users integrate AI on top of existing workflows ("I ask Claude to write this email") rather than embedding AI at the point of information capture ("all incoming information is processed by an AI layer before I touch it"). Claude Code Lab exists to teach the architectural shift, not the tools.

**Framework core:**
- **The 73% trap:** Tool-first integration without workflow redesign → no structural time reduction → tool abandoned
- **The 27% pattern:** Workflow-first design → one concrete implementation where AI processes information at capture → compounds over time → tool becomes infrastructure
- **The curriculum implication:** Session 1 should name this explicitly. Students arrive with tool-first mental models. The first session plants the workflow-first model.
- **The content implication:** Every Claude Code Lab post that names the 73% stat positions the cohort as the solution

**Why this matters for the vault:** This is the strongest positioning argument for the paid cohort that has emerged in 2026. It is not in any framework, any marketing file, or any sales document.

**Candidate file:** `frameworks/AI Workflow Abandonment Pattern.md`

### 2. Personal AI Twin — Curriculum Reframe

**The idea:** The skills library session — where students accumulate 50+ reusable skills over 6 weeks — is already delivering a "personal AI twin." The session just isn't named that way. Renaming it unlocks a more compelling marketing hook and aligns with vocabulary now entering the LinkedIn Learning / LinkedIn mainstream.

**Proposed change:** Not a new framework — a language update across the curriculum outline, the landing page, and the carousels that describe the cohort arc. "By the end of 6 weeks you'll have 50+ skills: a digital twin that knows your workflow."
