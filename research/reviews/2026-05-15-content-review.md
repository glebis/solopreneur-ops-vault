## Content Trends

### LinkedIn Algorithm — New Signal: Saves Outrank Likes 5:1

The May 1 review covered dwell time and the 360 Brew AI shift. One signal not previously documented in the vault:

**Saves are now the strongest single engagement signal.** LinkedIn data shows saves drive 5× more reach than likes and 2× more reach than comments. The algorithm interprets a save as "this person wants to return to this post," which is a high-confidence quality signal.

**Implication for content design:** Posts should be designed to be *saveable* — reference material, frameworks, step-by-step guides, checklists — not just likeable. A "liked" post spreads for 48 hours; a "saved" post gets re-surfaced by the algorithm weeks later when the user returns to it.

**Current vault gap:** No framework addresses save-optimised content design. The Hook Patterns framework focuses on stopping the scroll; the Calendar framework focuses on scheduling. Neither addresses what makes a post worth saving.

### LinkedIn Algorithm — "Link in First Comment" Workaround Confirmed Penalized

The May 1 review documented that posts with external links in the body see ~60% reach reduction, and recommended keeping links in the first comment. **This workaround is now also penalized as of early 2026.**

LinkedIn's 360 Brew AI detects the link-in-comment pattern and applies the same distribution penalty as body links. The correct strategy is now:

- **Deep content with links → LinkedIn Newsletter** (bypasses the feed algorithm entirely)
- **Demos and tutorials → native video** (no link needed; CTA in caption)
- **Frameworks and how-tos → PDF carousel** (native format, highest engagement at 6.60%)
- **Only link in posts when the post's entire purpose is the link** (and accept the reach penalty knowingly)

**Action required:** Rule 2 in [[frameworks/LinkedIn Content Calendar]] is now outdated. Updated below.

### LinkedIn Algorithm — Topic DNA Locks In Faster Than Expected

LinkedIn's 360 Brew AI builds a "topic DNA" for each creator based on their posting history. Distribution shifts from network-based to interest-based within 4–6 weeks of consistent posting on a specific topic. This is faster than previously estimated.

**Implication:** Posting inconsistently (which the 14 missed daily tasks represent) doesn't just lose individual reach — it signals topic DNA drift and reduces the algorithm's confidence in the creator's niche. Consistency now matters algorithmically, not just for audience trust.

### AI Education — Specificity Is the 2026 Edge

Trend data shows the AI education market continues growing (projected $12.3B globally), but the differentiator has shifted. The market is now saturated with generic "AI for everyone" content. What's rising:

- **Hyper-specific use cases** with named professions and named outcomes ("how a Berlin UX designer cut client reporting time by 60%")
- **Multimodal demos** — screen recordings with narration outperform text-based tutorials in watch time and saves
- **Live cohort specifics** — CBC (cohort-based courses) achieve 85–90% completion vs. ~10% for self-paced. Sharing specific completion rates and cohort outcomes is a differentiator that no AI-generated content can replicate

The "Proof of Human Insight" concept from the May 1 review is now even more urgent. AI-generated educational content is flooding LinkedIn; posts that demonstrably contain lived experience are the scarcest signal.

### Solopreneur Content — Media Business First

The dominant framing for successful solopreneurs in 2026: **media business first, product business second.** Solo creators who treat distribution as the core competency and product as the extension are outperforming those who build first and promote later.

This aligns with [[frameworks/Infinite Sales Cycle]] (Create → Publish → Engage → Convert → Deliver → Document) but adds urgency: the Publish and Engage stages are not optional maintenance — they are the primary growth lever, and skipping them for 2 weeks (as the task backlog shows) costs algorithmic standing that takes 4–6 weeks to rebuild.

## New Concepts

### 1. Save-Optimised Content Design ("Saveable > Likeable")

**The idea:** LinkedIn's 360 Brew AI now weights saves at 5× the reach-value of likes. This means the most algorithmically effective content is not the most emotionally resonant post (gets likes) but the most *utility-dense* post (gets saved for later reference). A framework for designing "saveable" content.

**Core distinction:**
- **Likeable:** Story, opinion, emotion, relatable struggle → instant reaction, short-lived reach
- **Saveable:** Framework, checklist, step-by-step guide, reference table → deferred use, long-tail reach

**Saveable post patterns:**
- "The X-step process I use every time I [do Y]" → procedural, referenceable
- "The 3 questions I ask before [decision]" → decision support, worth re-reading
- Framework posts with named concepts → people save the terminology
- Comparison tables → reference material with ongoing utility

**Measurement:** Track saves-per-impression as a leading indicator; compare save rate of framework posts vs. story posts over 30 days.

**Candidate file:** `frameworks/Saveable Content Design.md`

### 2. Topic DNA Cultivation

**The idea:** LinkedIn's 360 Brew AI assigns each creator a "topic DNA" and routes new content to users interested in those topics — bypassing pure network distribution. This DNA is built from posting history and can be deliberately cultivated or accidentally diluted.

**Principles:**
- Post on 1–2 tightly related topics for 4–6 weeks to establish topic DNA; broader variety dilutes it
- DNA is built from content *and* comments — engaging on off-topic posts signals topic ambiguity
- Consistency of posting window (same days/times) reinforces the DNA signal
- Recovery after a gap takes 4–6 weeks of consistent on-topic posting

**For an AI educator:** Topic DNA should cluster tightly around "AI tools for non-developers" and "Claude Code for professionals" — not "AI in general" or "productivity tips." Every post and every comment should pass the filter: does this reinforce or dilute the topic DNA?

**Candidate file:** `frameworks/Topic DNA Cultivation.md`
