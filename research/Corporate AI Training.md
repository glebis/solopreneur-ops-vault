# Corporate AI Training — Selling B2B as a Solo Educator

> The fastest path from €4K/mo solopreneur to €7K+/mo: one corporate deal per quarter changes everything.

## Market Size

### Europe Corporate Training

The Europe corporate training market is valued at **$33.08 billion** in 2026 with a 7.6% CAGR through 2033. AI-specific corporate training is a subset growing much faster — the global AI in Corporate Training market is **$1.5B (2024)** heading to **$10.4B by 2033** at 25% CAGR.

### European AI Spending

European AI spending will reach **$290 billion by 2029** (33.7% CAGR). Key sectors: banking, retail, software, healthcare. 91% of companies plan to increase AI spending in L&D in 2026.

### The DACH Opportunity

Germany's data & AI services market grew **13%+ in 2024** alone. DACH businesses are ~18 months behind US/UK in AI adoption — but 2026 is the critical window because the EU AI Act requirements are now in effect and early-adopter ROI data is clear enough to eliminate "wait and see."

## How Corporations Buy Training

### The 6-Step Procurement Process

1. **Collect training data** — HR/L&D identifies skill gaps via surveys, performance reviews
2. **Analyze needs** — macro-level analysis: how many people, what skills, what urgency
3. **Develop strategy** — long-term plan, often tied to digital transformation initiatives
4. **Issue RFP** — request for proposals to 3-5 vendors (or direct outreach to known experts)
5. **Select partner** — evaluate proposals, often including a pilot/demo session
6. **Manage relationship** — ongoing value tracking, renewals, expansion

### Decision Makers

| Role | What They Care About | How to Reach |
|------|---------------------|-------------|
| **Head of L&D** | Skill gap closure, completion rates, employee satisfaction | LinkedIn content, L&D conferences |
| **CHRO / VP HR** | Talent retention, employer brand, compliance | Executive networking, referrals |
| **CTO / VP Engineering** | Technical depth, practical outcomes, speed to competence | Tech conferences, GitHub presence |
| **Innovation Lead** | Cutting-edge tools, competitive advantage, speed | LinkedIn thought leadership, demos |
| **Procurement** | Cost per person, vendor credentials, references | Formal proposals, case studies |

### Budget Cycles

- **Q4** (Oct-Dec): Budget planning for next year. Best time to plant seeds.
- **Q1** (Jan-Mar): New budgets released. Highest probability of closing.
- **Q2** (Apr-Jun): Mid-year spending. Good for "use it or lose it" budgets.
- **Q3** (Jul-Sep): Slowest. Use for relationship building and pipeline development.

### Budget Reality

Many L&D departments have surprisingly unclear budgets: 29% have no specific training budget, 31% aren't sure what it is. This means individual champions often find budget from multiple sources (L&D, innovation, team budgets).

### Pricing Strategy for Claude Code Lab

Current consumer pricing: €950/person for 6-week cohort.

**Corporate pricing should be:**
- **Team workshop (half-day):** €2,500 — intro to AI agents, hands-on with Claude Code
- **Team program (6 weeks, 3-5 people):** €5,000-7,500 — full lab experience with dedicated support
- **Enterprise (10+ people):** €1,200/person — volume discount, custom curriculum
- **Keynote + workshop combo:** €4,000-6,000 — conference speaking + hands-on afternoon

**Key insight:** Most training requires 1-3 days of preparation per delivery day. Factor this into pricing. A 2-day workshop with 3 days prep should be priced at minimum €6,250 (5 days × €1,250/day).

### The Math

One corporate deal per quarter at €5,000 = **€20,000/year** in B2B revenue. Combined with consumer cohorts and community, this pushes past the €7,000/mo comfort target.

## Selling Without a Sales Team

### LinkedIn-First Strategy (Primary Channel)

From [[research/LinkedIn Strategy]]:
- 60% of B2B buyers discover brands through creator content before filling out a form
- Post about corporate AI transformation challenges 1x/week
- Share anonymized case studies from lab participants working in corporate contexts
- Engage with L&D and innovation leaders' posts

### The 5 Paths to Corporate Deals

1. **Alumni referral** — "My team needs this too." Easiest path, highest conversion. From [[frameworks/Alumni as Marketing Engine]].
2. **LinkedIn inbound** — Decision maker sees your content, reaches out. Takes 3-6 months of consistent posting.
3. **Conference speaking** — Present at AI/tech events, offer post-talk workshops. From [[research/Partnership Channels]].
4. **Consultancy partnership** — Boutique consultancies need AI training modules. You deliver, they sell.
5. **Direct outreach** — Cold approach to innovation/L&D leaders at target companies. Lowest conversion, highest volume.

### Conference Speaking ROI

The Facilitator School case study showed 56% capture rate at conferences (339 signups from 600 attendees). For corporate, even a 5% conversion rate from a 200-person talk = 10 warm leads = 2-3 corporate deals.

## Format Preferences

### What Corporations Want

| Format | Demand | Best For |
|--------|--------|----------|
| **Half-day workshop** | Highest | Intro/awareness, large groups (20-50) |
| **Multi-week cohort** | Growing | Deep skill building, small teams (5-10) |
| **Lunch & learn** | Medium | Low-commitment entry point, pipeline building |
| **Self-paced + mentoring** | Medium | Scaling across time zones, large orgs |
| **Full-day intensive** | Medium | When travel budget is available |
| **Ongoing retainer** | Low but highest value | Post-training support, embedded advisor |

### Remote vs In-Person

- Remote programs: lower friction, wider reach, lower pricing
- In-person workshops: higher perceived value, stronger relationships, easier to charge premium
- Hybrid (Berlin + remote): in-person kickoff + remote follow-up is the emerging best practice

### The Winning Format for a Solo Educator

**"Taster + Deep Dive" model:**
1. Free 30-min lunch & learn (builds pipeline)
2. Paid half-day workshop (€2,500, proves value)
3. 6-week team program (€5,000-7,500, deep engagement)
4. Ongoing community access (€49/person/mo, retention)

## Proposal Template

### Structure of a Winning Corporate Training Proposal

```markdown
# AI Agent Training Program — [Company Name]

## Executive Summary (1 paragraph)
What we'll do, for whom, and what outcome to expect.

## The Challenge
Why AI agent skills matter for [their specific industry/role].
Specific pain points identified in discovery call.

## Program Design
- Format: [workshop / multi-week / hybrid]
- Duration: [timeline]
- Participants: [number, roles]
- Delivery: [in-person Berlin / remote / hybrid]

## Curriculum Overview
Week-by-week or session-by-session breakdown.
Tailored to their tools and workflows.

## Expected Outcomes
- Specific, measurable skills gained
- Tools participants will be able to use independently
- Before/after scenario for their daily work

## Trainer Profile
20 years IT, 350+ alumni, 50+ AI skills built.
Notable projects and credentials.

## Social Proof
- 2-3 relevant case studies or testimonials
- Cohort completion rate (96% for cohort-based)
- Alumni outcomes

## Investment
- Pricing options (per-person / flat fee / tiers)
- What's included (materials, recordings, follow-up)
- Optional: community access add-on

## Next Steps
- Pilot session (free or discounted)
- Timeline to start
- Contact information
```

### Proposal Tips

- Lead with their problem, not your credentials
- Include 2-3 pricing tiers (anchoring effect)
- Offer a free pilot session to reduce risk
- Use specific numbers from past cohorts
- Send within 24 hours of discovery call
