# LinkedIn Hook Patterns

> The first 2 lines determine whether anyone reads the rest. Here are 5 patterns that work for AI educators.

## Pattern 1: "I Did X, Here's What Happened"

**Template:** "I [specific action] for [duration/audience]. Here's what [surprising result]."

**Example:**
> I gave Claude Code to 8 non-technical people for 6 weeks.
> Here's what they built (it shocked me).

**Why it works:** Specific numbers + curiosity gap. The reader wants to know what happened.

## Pattern 2: The Contrarian

**Template:** "Stop [common advice]. Here's what [actually matters]."

**Example:**
> Stop learning prompt engineering.
> Here's what matters instead in 2026.

**Why it works:** Challenges assumptions. People click to disagree or learn.

## Pattern 3: The How-To

**Template:** "How to [desirable outcome] in [surprisingly short time] ([constraint])."

**Example:**
> How to go from idea to deployed website in 15 minutes.
> No code required.

**Why it works:** Clear value proposition. The constraint (no code) qualifies the audience.

## Pattern 4: The Mistake

**Template:** "I [embarrassing mistake] for [duration]. The fix was [simple]."

**Example:**
> I wasted 3 months building features nobody needed.
> The fix was embarrassingly simple: I asked 5 people what they actually wanted.

**Why it works:** Vulnerability + lesson. People relate to mistakes more than successes.

## Pattern 5: The Number

**Template:** "[Impressive number]. [Context]. [One lesson]."

**Example:**
> 50 AI skills. 350 alumni. 1 lesson I keep learning:
> people don't need more tools. They need fewer choices.

**Why it works:** Numbers anchor credibility. One lesson = memorable takeaway.

## The 80/20 Rule

**80% educational/entertaining, 20% promotional.**

Your selling posts should feel like natural extensions of teaching:
- Teaching post → "If you want to learn this, next cohort starts June 2"
- Proof post → "Here's what [alumni name] built in week 3"
- Never: "Buy my course! Limited spots!"

## Content Mix (Weekly)

| Day | Type | Hook Pattern |
|-----|------|-------------|
| Tuesday | Teaching | How-To or Contrarian |
| Thursday | Proof | "I Did X" or Number |
| Saturday | Conversation | Contrarian or Mistake |
