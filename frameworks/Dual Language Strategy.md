# Dual Language Strategy

> One language per platform. LinkedIn = English. Telegram = Russian. Don't dilute.

## The Principle

Bilingual creators who try to serve both audiences on one platform dilute both. The research is clear: **pick one primary language per platform.**

## Platform Split

| Platform | Language | Audience | Content |
|----------|----------|----------|---------|
| **LinkedIn** | English | Western/international market | Teaching, proof, sales |
| **Telegram** | Russian | CIS/Russian-speaking community | Community, announcements, discussions |
| **YouTube** | English (Russian subtitles) | Both markets | Demos, tutorials |
| **Astro Site** | English | SEO, international | Frameworks, research, vault |

## Transition Plan (4-6 Weeks)

### Week 1-2: Signal the shift
- Pin a post: "Expanding internationally — posting in English from now on. Russian content stays in [Telegram link]"
- Start posting 100% in English on LinkedIn
- Continue Russian in Telegram

### Week 3-4: Build new habits
- Engage only in English on LinkedIn
- Connect with English-speaking AI educators, founders, CTOs
- Russian-speaking connections who value your content will read English

### Week 5-6: Solidify
- Update LinkedIn profile to English-only
- Set up secondary Russian language profile
- Remove any mixed-language posts from Featured section

## Why This Works

- Russian-speaking professionals in tech read English fluently
- New English-speaking connections won't be confused by mixed feeds
- Algorithm rewards consistency in topic AND language
- Telegram community stays engaged with dedicated Russian content

## Anti-Patterns

- Posting the same content in both languages on LinkedIn (confusing)
- Translating every post (double the work, half the impact)
- Switching languages mid-thread in comments
- Using English on Telegram (your Russian community chose Telegram for a reason)
