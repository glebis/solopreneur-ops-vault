# Behavioral Activation

> Action first. Motivation follows. Not the other way around.

## The Principle

Behavioral Activation (BA) comes from CBT research on depression: people who act *despite* not feeling motivated break the cycle faster than those who wait for motivation to arrive.

For solopreneurs, this translates directly:

```
Traditional:  Motivation → Action → Result
BA approach:  Small Action → Result → Motivation → Bigger Action
```

## Applied to Daily Operations

### The 5-Minute Rule
If a task takes under 5 minutes, do it now. No list, no planning, no overthinking.

**Examples in this vault:**
- Reply to one LinkedIn comment (2 min)
- Share one skill demo link (1 min)
- Review agent research output (3 min)
- Send one follow-up message (2 min)

### Activity Scheduling (not to-do lists)
BA uses *scheduled activities*, not infinite task lists. The dashboard shows exactly 5 items because:
- 5 is scannable in under 10 seconds
- At least one is completable in 5 minutes (guaranteed quick win)
- At least one is already done by an agent (progress without effort)
- Remaining items are pre-prioritized — no decision fatigue

### Avoidance Mapping
Common avoidance patterns for this project:

| Avoidance Behavior | What I'm Actually Avoiding | BA Response |
|---|---|---|
| Reading Telegram/news | Making a sales-related action | Do the 5-min item first, *then* Telegram |
| Coding a new skill | Promoting existing skills | Publish before building |
| Researching tools | Contacting a lead | Send one message, then research |
| Perfecting content | Shipping imperfect content | Ship today, improve tomorrow |

### Reward Tracking
BA emphasizes tracking *actions taken*, not outcomes. The vault tracks:
- Days with at least one visibility action ✓
- Streak of consecutive action days
- Agent tasks reviewed (not created)
