# JTBD Applied to Sales

> Your customers don't buy a lab. They hire it to do a job.

## The Buyer's Job

Nobody wakes up thinking "I need a 6-week Claude Code lab." They think:

> "When I see competitors shipping AI features 10x faster, I want to understand what agents can do for my work, so I can stop feeling left behind and start building."

## Switch Forces in Your Sales Conversations

Use these to understand where a lead is in their decision:

### Push (what frustrates them today)
- "I keep hearing about AI agents but don't know where to start"
- "My team tried ChatGPT but it felt like a toy"
- "Competitors are doing things with AI that I can't"
- "I spend too much time on tasks AI should handle"

### Pull (what attracts them to the lab)
- "Hands-on from day one, not just theory"
- "Non-developers can participate"
- "Real tools, real workflows, not just prompting"
- "350+ alumni, proven format"

### Habit (what keeps them from buying)
- "I'll figure it out on my own" (YouTube, docs)
- "I'm too busy for a 6-week commitment"
- "My team isn't technical enough"
- "We already use ChatGPT"

### Anxiety (what scares them about buying)
- "What if I can't keep up?"
- "Is €950 worth it for something I might not use?"
- "What if AI tools change and this becomes outdated?"
- "Will this actually work for my specific use case?"

## How to Use in Sales Conversations

1. **Listen for push signals** — frustration = readiness
2. **Amplify pull** — show concrete outcomes from alumni
3. **Reduce habit** — "YouTube doesn't give you feedback or a cohort"
4. **Address anxiety directly** — "3 difficulty levels, recordings available"

## Objection → Response Map

| Objection | JTBD Response |
|-----------|--------------|
| "Too expensive" | "What's the cost of 6 more months without this skill?" |
| "No time" | "2 sessions/week, 30 min/day practice. Less than your daily Telegram time." |
| "Not technical" | "40% of alumni are non-developers. That's the point." |
| "AI changes too fast" | "We teach agent thinking, not tool tricks. The framework survives tool changes." |
| "I'll learn on my own" | "Most people who say this are still saying it 6 months later." |
