# Newsletter Authority Channel

> LinkedIn newsletters bypass the feed algorithm entirely. Every subscriber receives a triple notification: email, push alert, and in-app. The algorithm that decides who sees a post does not govern who receives a newsletter — every subscriber gets it.

## Why This Matters

Regular LinkedIn posts reach **5–7% of followers** (algorithm-gated). A LinkedIn newsletter delivers to **100% of subscribers**, with **25–35% open rates**. One newsletter issue reaches more of your audience than 5–7 standard posts combined.

Additional signal from LinkedIn 360 Brew AI (2026): newsletter reach has grown ~48% under the current algorithm. LinkedIn actively promotes newsletter growth as a platform strategy. This is a first-mover window before newsletter saturation in any niche.

**Newsletter subscribers convert to buyers at 2–3× the rate of regular post readers.** The inbox context is different from the feed context — someone who opted into a newsletter is more invested than someone who happened to scroll past a post.

## Setup

1. Go to LinkedIn Creator settings → Newsletters → Create Newsletter
2. Name it to reflect topic DNA: "Claude Code Lab Dispatch" or "AI for Non-Developers Weekly" or "The Solopreneur Ops Letter"
3. Set cadence: 2x/month (aligns with biweekly review cycle; easily seeded from review output)
4. Write a short "what this newsletter is" description that mirrors your LinkedIn About section (profile-alignment signal)

**Time to setup:** 10 minutes. **Time to first issue:** 30 minutes using biweekly review output as raw material.

## Content Fit

| Content Type | Fits Newsletter | Why |
|---|---|---|
| AI framework breakdowns | Yes | Long-form; needs full reading context |
| Claude Code Lab round-ups | Yes | Cohort-based narrative; builds anticipation |
| "What I learned this cohort" | Yes | Personal, longitudinal, subscriber-appropriate |
| Tool analysis / comparisons | Yes | Depth that doesn't compress well into a post |
| Hot takes / contrarian opinions | No | Posts. Newsletter readers want substance, not provocation |
| Carousels, demos | No | Native post formats; newsletter is text-based long-form |

## Cadence and Seeding

**2x/month** aligns with the biweekly review cycle. The review already generates newsletter-worthy material: content trends, framework updates, and actionable recommendations are natural newsletter content with zero additional research.

**Minimum viable newsletter issue (first issue):**
- 300–500 words
- One insight from the latest biweekly review, expanded
- One "what to apply this week" section
- One CTA: link to cohort waitlist or Linktree

## Subscriber-Building Pattern

| Action | Where |
|---|---|
| Every PDF carousel CTA | "Subscribe to the newsletter for the full framework" |
| LinkedIn bio | Newsletter link as primary URL (not landing page) |
| Posts that go deep | "I write more on this in [Newsletter Name] — link in bio" |
| After every cohort | Graduates get invited to newsletter as ongoing alumni resource |
| Biweekly review → newsletter | Best insight from review becomes the newsletter lede |

## Common Mistakes

- **Too long:** Newsletter subscribers skim first, read if hooked. Lead with the single most important insight, not preamble.
- **Publishing to newsletter only:** Post a teaser on the feed that links to the newsletter (first comment if linking is needed). Build dual-distribution from the start.
- **No CTA:** Every issue should have one low-friction CTA. Not "buy now" — "reply to this with your biggest challenge" or "subscribe if a colleague sent this to you."
- **Wrong cadence:** Monthly is too infrequent to build a habit. Weekly may overwhelm. 2x/month is the production-sustainable default.
