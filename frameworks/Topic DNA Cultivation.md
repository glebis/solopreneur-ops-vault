# Topic DNA Cultivation

> LinkedIn 360Brew routes your content to people who already follow your topic — not just to your network. Narrow is more reach, not less.

## The Core Mechanism

LinkedIn's 360Brew algorithm learns what you post about and builds an interest graph around it. When you post about a narrow topic consistently, the algorithm routes each new post to an established audience of people interested in that topic — including people outside your network.

When you post across many topics, 360Brew has no interest graph to route to. Each post reaches only your direct followers, and none of it compounds.

```
Narrow topic + consistent posting
    → interest graph forms (4–6 weeks)
    → algorithm routes new posts to topic audience
    → reach expands beyond your network
    → topic authority compounds each post

Wide topic + inconsistent posting
    → no interest graph forms
    → each post reaches only your followers
    → reach stagnates regardless of quality
```

**Counter-intuition:** Narrowing your topic feels like talking to fewer people. The algorithm routes narrower content to *more* relevant people. A post about "Claude Code for non-developers" reaches a larger relevant audience than a post about "AI trends."

## Defining Your Topic DNA

Topic DNA is the 2–3 concept cluster that describes what you uniquely know and consistently teach. It should be:

- **Narrow enough** to form an interest graph (not "AI" — "Claude Code for non-developer practitioners")
- **Specific enough** to be routeable (not "productivity" — "AI workflow design for solopreneurs")
- **Authentic enough** to sustain 12 months of posting without burnout

**For Claude Code Lab:**

| Layer | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Core topic | Claude Code for non-developers |
| Context | Solopreneur and practitioner workflows |
| Perspective | System design before tool selection |
| Proof | 350+ alumni, 50+ skills built |

Every post should touch at least two of these four layers to stay within topic DNA.

## Topic Drift — The Distribution Killer

Topic drift happens when a creator chases trending topics outside their DNA to capture impressions. The short-term result: one viral post. The long-term result: the interest graph resets, and subsequent posts on the original topic underperform.

**Signs of drift:**
- Posting about a trending news story that has no natural connection to your topic
- Writing about a tool you don't use because it's getting traction in feeds
- Covering a business concept that's adjacent but not inside your practitioner experience

**Rule:** If you can't connect a topic to your own direct experience in Claude Code Lab (a session observation, an alumni outcome, a tool interaction), don't post it.

## Content Audit Criteria

Use this table quarterly to audit whether your last 12 posts are on-DNA:

| Post | Core topic? | Context layer? | Perspective present? | Personal proof? |
|------|------------|---------------|---------------------|----------------|
| ✓ on DNA | Yes | Yes | Yes | Yes or N/A |
| ⚠ borderline | Yes | No | Weak | No |
| ✗ off DNA | No | No | No | No |

Target: 10/12 posts on-DNA. If you're below 8/12, the interest graph is drifting.

## Content Types That Build Topic DNA (Ranked)

| Format | DNA Signal | Why |
|--------|-----------|-----|
| Original observation from a live session | Very high | Specificity, proof, personal experience in one post |
| Alumni result / case study | Very high | Social proof + topic specificity |
| Framework breakdown (PDF carousel) | High | Demonstrates expertise depth, saves reinforce topic |
| Contrarian take on common AI advice | High | Signals expertise, generates on-topic comments |
| Reaction to trending AI news | Medium | Depends on whether you connect to personal experience |
| Generic "X things about AI" list | Low | Widens topic surface; no DNA signal |

## Hashtag Alignment

Hashtags are a topic signal to 360Brew, not a reach driver. Use only hashtags that are inside your topic DNA. Three or fewer per post.

For Claude Code Lab: `#claudecode` · `#aiworkflow` · `#nondeveloper`
Avoid: `#ai` · `#productivity` · `#tech` · `#entrepreneur` (too wide, no DNA signal)

## The Topic Authority Timeline

| Phase | Posts | What's Happening |
|-------|-------|-----------------|
| Establishing | 1–20 | 360Brew is learning your topic; reach is network-only |
| Building | 20–50 | Interest graph forming; occasional out-of-network reach |
| Authority | 50–100+ | Consistent out-of-network distribution; topic owns you in the algorithm |

**Implication:** Topic authority is not built in a campaign. It compounds over months of consistent, narrow posting. The decision to narrow is a 6-month investment in distribution, not a one-post experiment.
