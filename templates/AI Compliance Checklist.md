# AI Literacy Compliance Checklist (EU AI Act Art. 4)

> Use as a workshop handout, lead magnet, or self-assessment tool.

## For Organizations Using AI Systems

### 1. Awareness (Required by Art. 4)

- [ ] Staff who interact with AI systems can explain what the system does
- [ ] Staff understand the limitations of AI outputs they use
- [ ] Staff know when to escalate AI decisions to a human
- [ ] Staff can identify when AI output seems incorrect or biased

### 2. Documentation

- [ ] AI systems in use are inventoried (what, where, who uses it)
- [ ] Risk level assessed for each AI system (minimal/limited/high/unacceptable)
- [ ] Data processing documented (what data enters the AI, what comes out)
- [ ] Human oversight procedures defined for high-risk decisions

### 3. Training

- [ ] AI literacy training provided to all staff interacting with AI
- [ ] Training covers: capabilities, limitations, risks, appropriate use
- [ ] Training is documented (who, when, what was covered)
- [ ] Refresher training scheduled (AI capabilities change rapidly)

### 4. Governance

- [ ] AI use policy published internally
- [ ] Incident reporting procedure defined (what if AI causes harm?)
- [ ] Regular review cycle established (quarterly recommended)
- [ ] Compliance responsibility assigned to a named person

## Non-Compliance Risk

- Fines up to **€7.5 million** or **1.5% of global turnover**
- Enforcement begins **August 2, 2026**
- Applies to ALL organizations using AI, not just AI developers
